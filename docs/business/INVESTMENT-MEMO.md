# Investment Memo

**Perspective:** Due diligence — company risk, not product design  
**Status:** Internal — investor conversation framework  
**Date:** 2026-07-20  

---

## Thesis

RedFace is **not** building another security software platform.

It is attempting to become the **operational coordination infrastructure** for organizations that execute real-world missions.

Security is the first reference implementation because the coordination problem is acute, measurable, and economically valuable.

If successful, the same kernel supports additional operational domains.

**That is an attractive thesis.**

---

## What Is Strong

### 1. First Principles, Not Features

Most startups begin with features. RedFace started with doctrine — and the doctrine is internally consistent.

### 2. The Correct Abstraction

Cameras are not the product. Dispatch is not the product. AI is not the product.

The abstraction:

```text
Intent → Mission → Events → History
```

That generalizes. Foundational platforms need exactly this.

### 3. Layered Architecture

```text
Identity → Policy → Mission → Event → History → Intelligence → Economy
```

Every layer has a reason to exist.

### 4. Distribution Thinking

Control room wedge — not selling to everyone. Exactly the kind of wedge investors want.

---

## Biggest Remaining Risks

### Risk 1 — Is the pain severe enough?

People don't buy architecture. They buy relief.

**Diligence question:** Do operations managers genuinely feel enough pain to change workflow?

**How we answer:** Customer discovery + reference deployment scorecard. Not demos — measured Before/After.

### Risk 2 — Switching costs

Control rooms already have software, radios, SOPs, cameras, reporting, staff habits.

**Requirement:** Fit in. Don't replace everything. Adapter strategy (translate → RSP) points the right direction.

**How we answer:** [Reference Deployment Playbook](../deployments/REFERENCE-DEPLOYMENT-PLAYBOOK.md) — parallel run, one mission class first.

### Risk 3 — Data ownership

Who owns mission history, playback, evidence, intelligence, derived insights when multiple organizations participate?

**How we answer:** [Data Ownership & Commercial Framework](DATA-OWNERSHIP.md)

### Risk 4 — Procurement

Security companies don't adopt new infrastructure overnight. Sales cycles are long.

**Mitigation:** Short path to first **paid** deployment — one mission class, one control room, simulator before hardware, evidence case study for internal buyer approval.

## Strategic Differentiator: Playback

Not AI. Not cameras. Not dashboards.

**Mission playback** — replay any mission exactly as it happened.

Value: audits, insurance, litigation, training, post-incident review, regulatory compliance.

Emerges naturally from the event model. Build the best operational playback in the industry.
