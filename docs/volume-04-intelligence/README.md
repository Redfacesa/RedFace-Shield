# Volume 4: The Intelligence Engine

**Status:** Planned  
**Prerequisites:** Volumes −1 through 3, Theory of Missions, Operational Graph

---

## Scope (Planned)

- Intelligence observes history (Principle Seven) — AI reads mission history, not raw feeds
- Mission optimization: resource matching, objective sequencing, duration prediction
- Security Knowledge Graph queries across mission relationships
- Pattern detection: connected missions, predictive patrol, anomaly detection
- From "what happened" to "why it happened" — operational intelligence
- Structured operational facts vs. raw media
- Lessons learned extraction from completed missions
- Cross-participant intelligence with privacy preservation

## Key Questions This Volume Will Answer

- How does AI optimize missions rather than incidents?
- How do lessons learned from one mission improve future missions across the network?
- What is the boundary between intelligence and surveillance?
- How are predictions communicated without overriding human authority?
